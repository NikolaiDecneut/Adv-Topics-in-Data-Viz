For a decade, gamers quietly switched teams

In 2014, four out of five gaming PCs ran an Intel processor. Today it is barely one in two. This is the story of how AMD clawed its way back from near-irrelevance.

Every month, millions of players let Valve quietly catalog the hardware inside their machines through the Steam Hardware Survey. Tracking the share of those PCs running Intel versus AMD processors turns that anonymous census into a 12-year tug-of-war — one with a clear turning point.

How to read this

The band is split top-to-bottom into the share of surveyed gaming PCs running each brand's CPU, so the two shares always add to 100%. The white dividing line is the front of the battle: as it slides down, AMD (bottom) is taking ground from Intel (top). Hover anywhere on the chart to read the exact split for that month.

Data: Valve Corporation, “Steam Hardware & Software Survey” (CPU vendor share, Jan 2014–Apr 2026), store.steampowered.com/hwsurvey. Monthly Intel/AMD shares were re-normalized to sum to 100% (a small “other” sliver was dropped). Cleaned data: cpu_market_share.csv.
