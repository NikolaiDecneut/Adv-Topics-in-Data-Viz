Apex Legends Review

A fast, fun game with a difficult movement skill gap

Image credit: EA / Respawn Entertainment

I recently started playing Apex Legends again, and I am loving it. I have been playing with a close friend, and we have been having a lot of fun. I am currently playing on PC, and I have realized that the skill I thought I had compared to the skill I actually have is quite different.

Right now, I am playing a rotation of three characters: Crypto, Valkyrie, and Loba. While I am enjoying the game again, there are a few things that get in the way of my own progression. The older physics engine allows players to develop movement techniques that are actually exploits rather than intentional mechanics.

Things like tap strafing and super gliding are visually impressive, but they also make it very difficult to track another player’s character. This creates a skill gap that may not have been intended by the developers. Because of this, I think there is an interesting debate about whether EA and Respawn Entertainment should create a possible “Apex Legends 2” on a more modern engine that can support intentional movement mechanics instead of relying on engine exploits.

My rating: 8/10
